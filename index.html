About Tyler, The Creator

Tyler Gregory Okonma—known around the world as Tyler, The Creator—is more than a rapper: he is a producer, designer, filmmaker, fashion pioneer, and a visionary for a new era[11][3][2]. From bold, pastel visuals to playful chaos and fearless self-expression, Tyler’s world is one of bright colors and sharper wit. Raised in LA, he co-founded Odd Future, carving an innovative lane with brash Odd Future tapes and his bleakly comic debut, Bastard. His ambitions quickly grew: Wolf layered in jazz and soulful joy, Cherry Bomb punctuated his love for punk and reckless sonics, and Flower Boy bloomed with introspection, bees, and heartbreak, marking a coming of age for both artist and fans[11][3].

IGOR pushed soulful synths, wigged-out alter egos, and heartbreak stories, while Call Me If You Get Lost and its Estate Sale expansion built a Wes Anderson-inspired travelogue aesthetic, with trunks, travel, and luxury. Chromakopia delivered lush, synth-heavy, throwback vibes, often with a noir twist. Don't Tap The Glass, his most recent, adds sparkling house/techno and visual storytelling—each era, a new world by design[3]. Whether in music, on stage, or via fashion icons like Golf Wang and Le Fleur *, Tyler creates immersive experiences that are bold, eclectic, and unapologetically unique.
